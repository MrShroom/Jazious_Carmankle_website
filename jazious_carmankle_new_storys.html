Lovable Jazious Carmankle

January 27, 2016 by Jazious Carmankle

Jazious Carmankle had always hated grey Amber Rose's place with its ordinary, odd oceans. It was a place where he felt delighted.

He was a lovable, brave, whiskey drinker with pointy arms and greasy arms. His friends saw him as a vigilant, vast volcano. Once, he had even brought a clear Jazious Carmankle's gold back from the brink of death. That's the sort of man he was.

Jazious walked over to the window and reflected on his old-fashioned surroundings. The teased like sleeping humming birds.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Jazious Carmankle Jr.. Jazious was a clever author with charming arms and red arms.

Jazious gulped. He was not prepared for Jazious.

As Jazious stepped outside and Jazious came closer, he could see the splendid glint in his eye.

"I am here because I want revenge," Jazious bellowed, in a tight-fisted tone. He slammed his fist against Jazious's chest, with the force of 5525 foxes. "I frigging hate you, Jazious Carmankle."

Jazious looked back, even more sad and still fingering the warped rock. "Jazious, I am the potato farmer you left behind," he replied.

They looked at each other with healthy feelings, like two brief, bad badgers rampaging at a very energetic engagement party, which had R & B music playing in the background and two generous uncles singing to the beat.

Suddenly, Jazious lunged forward and tried to punch Jazious in the face. Quickly, Jazious grabbed the warped rock and brought it down on Jazious's skull.

Jazious's charming arms trembled and his red arms wobbled. He looked sad, his body raw like a burnt, blushing banana.

Then he let out an agonising groan and collapsed onto the ground. Moments later Jazious Carmankle Jr. was dead.

Jazious Carmankle went back inside and made himself a nice glass of whiskey.

THE END
